# Bloomstead MVP Playtest Facilitator Worksheet

Use one copy of this worksheet for each observed first-session playtest. It is designed for 5–8 individual sessions and can be printed or completed digitally.

## How to use this worksheet

- Capture what happened before deciding what it means.
- Keep participant statements, direct observations, timing data, facilitator interventions, technical defects, and PM interpretation in their own sections.
- Use the participant’s own words whenever possible. Put exact quotes in **Verbatim participant statements**.
- Record an intervention at the moment it happens. Do not count a session as “without intervention” if a facilitator corrected, explained, or led the participant.
- Complete **PM interpretations** only after the session has ended.
- These are working research targets, not balance commitments or pass/fail requirements. Do not require Level 5 or Village Feast in every session.

## 2. Facilitator opening script

Read or adapt this before the participant begins:

> Thanks for trying Bloomstead. Today we are testing the game, not you. There are no right or wrong actions, and you are not being evaluated.
>
> Please think aloud as you play. Say what you notice, what you expect to happen, what you are trying to do, and what feels confusing or satisfying. It is okay to change your mind or make a mistake.
>
> I may stay quiet so I can see what the game communicates on its own. I will avoid teaching mechanics, answering gameplay questions, or steering you toward a particular action. If I do need to intervene, I will record exactly what I said and why.
>
> Please play as you normally would. If something does not work, tell me what you expected and what happened.

Facilitator reminder: neutral prompts such as “What are you looking at?” or “What would you try next?” are still interventions. Record them in the intervention log.
